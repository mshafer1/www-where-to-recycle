Habitat for Humanity in Searcy, Cabot, and Little Rock all accept anything except...

Mattresses and water beds

Doors with holes or rotten wood

incomplete toilets or toilets with cracks

Non-working appliences or appliences over 10 years old (can be accepted for scrap)

Computers, printers, copiers, fax machines, etc.

Outdated electrical items

Paint less than 3/4 full, rusted cans/lids, frozen paint/cans/lids, or commercial/oil based paint

Tires

Incomplete doorknobs or locks without keys

Lumber less than 4 feet or half a sheet, or with nails

Console or projection TVs, or TVs not having AV hookups

Textbooks and encyclopedias

Goodwill, area charities, and consignment stores

Various gently used housewares, electronics, clothing, toys, etc.

Downtown Church of Christ

Cardboard for their victory garden

Local Church Resource Rooms, Schools, and Daycares

toilet paper/paper towel rolls

buttons

left over stickers

fabric

crayons

glue

scissors

paper

plastic jugs, baby food jars, unique size glass jar, etc.

books

Toys (indoor and outdoor)

play props

children's furniture
